It was 1st of November 2018 & I was looking for a job switch. After work, I boarded a train from Santacruz and fortunately got a seat.

A gentleman (lets call him X) probably in his early 50s, sitting next to me on the train was watching a JavaScript video on Prototypes on his phone.
After a bit of hesitation, (I am not very good at striking conversations) I decided to talk to him.

He was one of the co-founders and a CTO of an early stage startup in Mumbai. As I come from a startup too, we had a lot in common
to discuss. We discussed a lot of things from technology to startups, from books to blogs. He had also worked with JP
Morgan for more than a decade on ETL systems so it was interesting to listen to his experience on that. It was a good conversation.

After X got off the train when his destination arrived, someone else said "Hello" from behind. I looked around, a man in his 30s was looking
at me. I replied "Hello". He quickly asked me "Are you a JavaScript developer?". After I replied positively he asked "Are you looking for a job switch?"

I said "Yes!".

I was still clueless and then I realized that he had overheard my conversation with X.

He handed over his business card to me. He was heading the Data Science team in one of the well funded and fast growing startups in Mumbai. He told me his company
is looking for some JavaScript developers and asked me to mail him my resume and other details. I thanked him and he got down at the next station.

I mailed him my resume the next day and he said he will forward it internally to the recruiters and will get back to me in a couple of days. We exchanged a few emails after that where he asked me about my preferences in technology stack & asked me to try out their product to get a better understanding about what they're trying to build.

I got a reply from him in a couple of days that someone from the Human Resources team will contact me and schedule an interview.

I got my telephonic interview scheduled on a Saturday. I was asked some basic questions on different topics such as REST Architecture, Node.js,
Python v/s Node.js, Django v/s Express and other similar questions. The interview lasted for about 45 minutes.

I was immediately informed that I have cleared the telephonic interview and will be given a programming assignment as the next step in the interview process. I had to complete the assignment in 3-4 days.

The assignment required me to build a complete full stack application(prototype), pushing the code to Github with frequent commits & deploying it live on a server.

I built the application using Node.js, Express.js for the backend APIs, Vanilla JavaScript, Bootstrap & some other libraries for the front end, MongoDB as my database which I
hosted on a remote Atlas Server. I finally deployed the application on Heroku.

After submitting the application I got a call for the final interview in their Mumbai office.

My final interview was scheduled on the upcoming friday. I was going to be interviewed by one of their senior full stack developers. The final interview consisted of a brief discussion on the programming assignment from the previous round, some improvements that could be added on the top of existing code
& other discussion on Node.js, JavaScript, some problems on Algorithms & Data Structures.

After a couple of days after the final interview, I was given a job offer.

I didn't end up joining there as I had other offers in hand but it ended up being an interesting experience to share!
